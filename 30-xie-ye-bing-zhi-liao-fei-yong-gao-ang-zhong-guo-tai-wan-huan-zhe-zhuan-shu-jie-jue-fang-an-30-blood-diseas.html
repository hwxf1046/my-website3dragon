一、痛点深度分析

在中国台湾，白血病、淋巴瘤等血液恶性肿瘤治疗周期长、费用高，普通家庭难以承受。

这一问题的根源在于：
1. 医疗资源分配不均：优质医疗资源集中在少数大型医院，基层医疗机构能力不足。
2. 医保政策限制：公共医疗保险覆盖范围有限，自费比例高，患者经济压力大。
3. 诊疗流程繁琐：从初诊到确诊再到治疗，环节众多，耗时漫长。
4. 专业人才短缺：特定领域的专科医生培养周期长，供需矛盾突出。
5. 技术创新滞后：新技术、新疗法临床应用审批流程复杂，患者难以及时获益。

对于中国台湾患者而言，这些问题不仅仅是医疗层面的挑战，更是生活质量、家庭负担、心理健康的多重考验。

  1. In-depth Pain Point Analysis

In Taiwan, Blood cancers like leukemia and lymphoma require long treatment cycles with high costs that ordinary families cannot afford.

The root causes of this issue include:
1. Uneven distribution of medical resources: High-quality medical resources are concentrated in a few large hospitals, while primary care institutions lack capabilities.
2. Health insurance policy restrictions: Public health insurance coverage is limited, out-of-pocket ratios are high, and patients face significant financial pressure.
3. Cumbersome diagnosis and treatment processes: From initial consultation to diagnosis and then to treatment, there are numerous steps that take a long time.
4. Shortage of specialized professionals: Training cycles for specialists in specific fields are long, and supply-demand contradictions are prominent.
5. Lagging technological innovation: Clinical application approval processes for new technologies and therapies are complex, making it difficult for patients to benefit timely.

For patients in Taiwan, these issues are not only medical challenges but also multiple tests of quality of life, family burden, and mental health.
