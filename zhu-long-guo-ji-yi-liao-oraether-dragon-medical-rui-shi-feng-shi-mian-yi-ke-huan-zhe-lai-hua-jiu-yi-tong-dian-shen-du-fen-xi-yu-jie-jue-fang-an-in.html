### Core Pain Point Analysis for Swiss Rheumatology and Immunology Patients

 

#### Pain Point 1: Extremely High Medical Costs

 

Although Switzerland has universal health insurance, the out-of-pocket ratio is high. Rheumatic and immune diseases require long-term medication, creating a heavy financial burden. Annual biologic treatment costs can reach CHF 30,000-50,000. Even with insurance coverage, patients' out-of-pocket expenses still amount to several thousand francs.

 

**Solution:** Treatment costs at top Chinese rheumatology and immunology departments are only 1/4 to 1/3 of those in Switzerland, while medical quality has reached international advanced levels. Aether Dragon Medical helps patients significantly reduce financial burden while ensuring treatment effectiveness.

 

#### Pain Point 2: Strict Restrictions on Biologic Agent Use

 

Switzerland has strict guideline restrictions on the use of biologic agents. Many patients cannot use these effective drugs because they don't meet the criteria and can only continue using traditional drugs with poorer effects, resulting in poor disease control.

 

**Solution:** Chinese rheumatology and immunology doctors can develop personalized treatment plans based on patients' specific conditions, with relatively flexible use of biologic agents. Aether Dragon Medical can assist patients in connecting with professional rheumatology departments to obtain more suitable treatment plans.

 

#### Pain Point 3: Difficulty in Diagnosing and Treating Rare Autoimmune Diseases

 

For rare autoimmune diseases such as systemic sclerosis, polymyositis, and vasculitis, the number of centers capable of diagnosis and treatment in Switzerland is limited. Patients often need to seek medical care across regions, with long diagnostic cycles.

 

**Solution:** Multiple Chinese tertiary hospitals have rare disease specialty clinics in their rheumatology and immunology departments, equipped with advanced autoantibody testing and imaging examination equipment. Aether Dragon Medical can assist patients in quickly completing comprehensive examinations to shorten diagnostic time.

 

#### Pain Point 4: Long Waiting Times for Specialist Doctors

 

The number of rheumatology and immunology specialist doctors in Switzerland is limited, with an average waiting time of 6-8 weeks from referral to seeing a specialist. For patients in the active phase of disease, this delay may lead to irreversible joint damage.

 

**Solution:** Rheumatology and immunology doctor resources in large Chinese hospitals are relatively sufficient, and specialist consultations can be arranged quickly. Aether Dragon Medical cooperates with domestic top rheumatology departments such as Peking Union Medical College Hospital Rheumatology and Immunology Department and Shanghai Renji Hospital Rheumatology Department to ensure patients receive timely diagnosis and treatment.

 

#### Pain Point 5: Inconvenient Long-term Follow-up Management

 

Rheumatic and immune diseases require long-term follow-up and medication adjustments, but some areas in Switzerland have a shortage of rheumatologists, making it difficult for patients to obtain timely follow-up visits and opportunities to adjust treatment plans.

 

**Solution:** Aether Dragon Medical provides remote follow-up management services, assisting patients in maintaining contact with domestic attending physicians, providing regular follow-up reminders, and assisting in arranging necessary examinations to ensure continuous optimization of treatment effects.
