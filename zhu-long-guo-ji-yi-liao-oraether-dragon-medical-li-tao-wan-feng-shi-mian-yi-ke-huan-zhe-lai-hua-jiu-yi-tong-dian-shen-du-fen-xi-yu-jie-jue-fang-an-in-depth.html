### Introduction

 

As a Baltic nation, Lithuania has relatively limited medical resources. In the field of rheumatology and immunology, strict restrictions on biologic agent use, shortage of specialist doctors, and insufficient capabilities for rare autoimmune disease diagnosis and treatment are becoming major troubles for Lithuanian rheumatology patients. Aether Dragon Medical specializes in providing end-to-end support for overseas patients seeking medical care in China.

 

### Core Pain Point Analysis

 

#### Pain Point 1: High Medical Costs

Rheumatic and immune diseases require long-term medication, with high annual biologic treatment costs. Even with insurance coverage, out-of-pocket expenses are heavy.

 

**Solution:** Treatment costs at top Chinese rheumatology departments are only 1/3 to 1/2 of those in Lithuania.

 

#### Pain Point 2: Strict Restrictions on Biologic Agent Use

Strict guideline restrictions on the use of biologic agents prevent many patients from using these effective drugs.

 

**Solution:** Chinese rheumatology doctors can develop personalized treatment plans based on patients' specific conditions.

 

#### Pain Point 3: Difficulty in Rare Autoimmune Disease Diagnosis and Treatment

Limited centers capable of diagnosing and treating rare autoimmune diseases such as systemic sclerosis and vasculitis.

 

**Solution:** Multiple Chinese tertiary hospitals have rare disease specialty clinics in their rheumatology departments.

 

#### Pain Point 4: Long Waiting Times for Specialist Doctors

Average waiting time from referral to seeing a specialist is 6-8 weeks.

 

**Solution:** Rheumatology doctor resources in large Chinese hospitals are sufficient, and specialist consultations can be arranged quickly.

 

#### Pain Point 5: Inconvenient Long-term Follow-up Management

Shortage of rheumatologists in some areas makes it difficult for patients to obtain timely follow-up visits.

 

**Solution:** Aether Dragon Medical provides remote follow-up management services.
