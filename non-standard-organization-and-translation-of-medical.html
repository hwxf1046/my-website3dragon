In-Depth Pain Point Analysis | 痛点深度分析

Primary Challenge: Non-Standard Organization and Translation of Medical Records

核心挑战：病历资料整理与翻译不规范

The healthcare landscape for international patients has become increasingly complex. Patients from Australia face unique challenges when navigating Chinas medical system:

1. COMMUNICATION BARRIERS
Previous medical records of international patients vary in format, lack unified standards, and translation quality is inconsistent, with key information missing or mistranslated.

2. SYSTEM NAVIGATION DIFFICULTIES
The Chinese healthcare system operates differently from Australia. Patients struggle with appointment booking procedures, department selection, insurance coordination, and understanding treatment protocols.

3. CULTURAL ADAPTATION ISSUES
Medical practices, patient-doctor interaction styles, and hospital environments in China may differ significantly from what patients from Australia are accustomed to, causing anxiety and discomfort.

4. LOGISTICAL COMPLEXITIES
Visa applications, accommodation arrangements, transportation, meal planning, and post-treatment care coordination create additional burdens for patients already dealing with health concerns.

5. INFORMATION ASYMMETRY
Lack of reliable information about hospital capabilities, expert qualifications, treatment success rates, and cost structures leads to suboptimal decision-making.

国际患者的医疗环境日益复杂。来自澳大利亚的患者在导航中国医疗系统时面临独特挑战：

1. 沟通障碍
国际患者的既往病历格式各异，缺乏统一标准，翻译质量参差不齐，关键信息遗漏或误译，影响中国医生的诊断判断和治疗方案制定。

2. 系统导航困难
中国医疗体系的运作方式与澳大利亚不同。患者在预约流程、科室选择、保险协调和理解治疗方案方面遇到困难。

3. 文化适应问题
中国的医疗实践、医患互动方式和医院环境可能与澳大利亚患者习惯的存在显著差异，导致焦虑和不适。

4. 后勤复杂性
签证申请、住宿安排、交通、餐饮规划和治疗后护理协调给已经面临健康问题的患者带来额外负担。

5. 信息不对称
缺乏关于医院能力、专家资质、治疗成功率和费用结构的可靠信息，导致决策不够优化。
